Sauteed Mushrooms

Though simple, sauteed mushrooms are one of my favorite things in the entire world. In a world where we have been blessed by a being as ubiquitous and intelligent as fungus, are we not to enjoy what they can offer to us? Mushrooms are a beautiful thing. They're not quite a vegetable but they're not quite not a vegetable. They're not quite meat but they're not quite not meat. And don't even get me started on the world of mycelium beneath our very feet. If there is a god, it is mushrooms.

If you made it this far without giving your screen a weird look and closing this tab, then congratulations! It's mushroom time.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of portobello mushrooms
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • As mush rosemary as your heart desires
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced

Directions

  1. Add butter and oil to a pan and heat on medium-high
  2. Once that is hot, add the mushrooms and soy sauce to the pan and toss the mushrooms with the mixture.
  3. Let it cook for about 5 minutes, and do not stir, Leave those bad boys to brown on one side.
  4. Add the garlic and rosemary and cook another 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Add salt and pepper to taste. You shouldn't need much salt because of the soy sauce, so be careful not to add too much.
